Concentrix · BPO Operations
Turning a 2–3 Day Reporting Lag Into Hourly KPI Visibility
⚠
The Problem

The site's KPI performance was declining, but the team only found out when the client sent rankings, typically with a 2–3 day lag. By the time leadership had the numbers, the coaching window had already closed. The operations team was reacting to a past they couldn't change, and it was directly affecting site bonus outcomes and client standing.

→
The Solution

I collaborated with the operations and quality teams to identify what signals were available in real time. My role was to design and build a dashboard that tracked hourly agent behavior and quality performance metrics, giving leadership immediate visibility into trends before they compounded. The goal wasn't just a dashboard; it was a shift in how the team operated: from waiting for external reports to monitoring their own data, on their own schedule.

✓
The Result

The site moved from reactive management to proactive, hourly performance monitoring. Coaching became targeted and timely. Accountability became visible across teams. The improvement in KPI consistency directly supported better site bonus outcomes and reinforced a culture where data clarity drove operational decisions, not the client's delayed report.

Reactive → Proactive management Hourly visibility from 2–3 day lag Site bonus improvement Structured accountability
New York Family Dentistry · Healthcare
Fixing a Broken Patient Identifier That Was Quietly Corrupting Reports
⚠
The Problem

The organization's reporting relied on Patient Chart IDs as the primary identifier, but during analysis, it became clear these IDs were sometimes missing or inconsistent across datasets. The downstream effect was inaccurate reporting outputs, duplicate patient records, and decisions being made on data that didn't reflect reality. Trust in the reporting pipeline was eroding.

→
The Solution

I reviewed the full data pipeline and traced the inconsistencies back to their source. Through the analysis, I discovered that the system actually contained a unique patient identifier that wasn't being used in reporting, more reliable, more consistent, and present across all datasets. I recommended transitioning the reporting logic to use this true unique patient ID, replacing the unreliable Chart ID as the primary key for patient record tracking.

✓
The Result

Switching to the unique patient ID eliminated duplicate records, reduced data inconsistencies, and restored trust in reporting outputs across the organization. Teams could now make operational decisions from standardized, accurate information, and the long-term reporting infrastructure became more reliable as a result. A structural fix, not a patch.

Root cause identified in pipeline Duplicate records eliminated Restored reporting trust Structural data integrity fix
